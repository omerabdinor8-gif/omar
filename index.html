Knowledge Management

How We Apply KM Processes

The Digital Entrepreneur Academy embeds Knowledge Management principles — discovery, capture, sharing, and application — throughout every aspect of learning and innovation.

🔍
KM Process 01

Knowledge Discovery

Students and lecturers continuously research emerging trends, academic literature, and industry developments to uncover new knowledge that strengthens the programme.

  • Research and information gathering from multiple sources
  • Identifying gaps in existing knowledge and practice
  • Exploring new digital business models and technologies
📋
KM Process 02

Knowledge Capture

All learning, insights, and discoveries are systematically documented through lecture materials, student portfolios, and digital repositories — turning tacit knowledge into explicit, reusable assets.

  • Organising and documenting course knowledge
  • Creating student project portfolios and case studies
  • Using LMS platforms like Moodle and Google Classroom
🤝
KM Process 03

Knowledge Sharing

Through collaborative projects, peer learning, and open academic discussions, students and lecturers exchange ideas that multiply the value of individual knowledge across the entire community.

  • Sharing ideas with classmates and lecturers in group work
  • Presentations, seminars, and academic discussions
  • Digital collaboration via Teams, Zoom, and shared platforms
⚡
KM Process 04

Knowledge Application

Acquired knowledge is applied directly to real business challenges — from building this very website as a KM final assessment to launching student startups and consulting projects in the market.

  • Applying knowledge to build the website and improve learning
  • Using insights to develop business plans and startup pitches
  • Continuous improvement through feedback and reflection
